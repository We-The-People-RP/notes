**# Economy / Job Balance Feedback

## Main Money-Making Jobs
According to the player, only 3 jobs currently matter financially:
1. Diving
2. Trucking
3. Hunting

Gold mining is considered decent but heavily group-dependent.

# Trucking
- Extremely profitable when few people are doing it.
- Around $1 million every ~4 hours under low competition.
- Profit drops when many players are trucking because players compete for higher-paying jobs/contracts.
- Considered one of the top consistent solo income methods.

## Core Issue
- Scaling/competition reduces profitability significantly.
- Job availability bottlenecks earnings.

# Hunting

## Earnings
- Around $250k in ~20 minutes initially.
- Profit slows afterward due to animal respawn timers.

## Variables Affecting Profit
- Animal spawn rates.
- Competition with other hunters.
- Use of bait:
  - Bait increases animal attraction/spawns.
  - Bait is expensive.

## Additional Notes
- Lions can kill players, adding risk.
- Used to be the dominant money-making activity.
- Profit decreases when multiple hunters share an area because spawn density is effectively split.

## Core Issue
- Spawn competition punishes player density.
- Respawn pacing reduces long-session viability.

# Diving

Shawn is highly experienced in this system:
- Diving level: 140+
- Jewelry level: 350+

## Current Economic Problems

### Gold Ore Oversupply
- Gold ore drops excessively.
- Gold ore massively outpaces jewelry part acquisition.
- Players accumulate huge amounts of unusable gold ingots.

### Example Provided
Full boat haul (~1500 total weight):
- ~2200–2300 gold ore
- Converts into ~1100–1150 gold ingots
- Jewelry parts only support ~500–600 usable pieces.

## Consequences
- Massive surplus gold.
- Jewelry parts become the true bottleneck.
- Silver becomes effectively worthless.
- Players constantly request jewelry parts from others.

# Material Economy Problems

## Copper
- Essentially useless because amber rarely drops.
- Main craftable item requiring copper:
  - Copper ring → requires amber.
- Copper lacks meaningful value.

## Silver
- Considered inefficient/worthless.
- Gold jewelry is always more profitable.
- Valuable jewelry parts are better spent on gold crafts.

# Diving Profit Structure

Diving income requires:
1. Diving time
2. Smelting time
3. Jewelry crafting time

## Major Complaint: Time Investment
Smelting and jewelry crafting are described as:
- Tedious
- Time-gated
- Unfun
- Widely disliked by the playerbase

### Example Workload
- 30+ hours diving
- 4–5 hours smelting
- 16 hours jewelry crafting in one session
- Additional 5–6 hour crafting session before that

## Important Insight
Diving itself is enjoyable/casual.
Processing materials afterward is what players hate.

# Suggested Improvements

## 1. Crafting/Smelting Skill Tree
Suggested system:
- Skill points reduce crafting/smelting timers.
- Jewelry value could be reduced slightly in exchange.

### Reasoning
- Players dislike sitting at benches/smelters for hours.
- This is allegedly why diving participation dropped sharply.

## 2. Improve Weak Jobs Instead of Nerfing Strong Ones
Player explicitly states:
- They do not want diving nerfed.
- They want weaker jobs improved.

### Core Philosophy
If one job is clearly superior:
- Players naturally converge on it.
- Other jobs become irrelevant.

# Social / QoL Feedback

## Diving Radios
- Radios no longer usable while diving.
- Diving used to be a social/group activity.
- Players dislike losing communication during dives.

Shawn personally does not care much about this change, but says many others strongly dislike it.

# Most Actionable Balance Issues

## High Priority
- Gold ore drop rates far too high.
- Jewelry parts too scarce.
- Silver economically irrelevant.
- Copper/amber progression broken.
- Crafting/smelting timers excessively long.
- Too few competitive money-making jobs.

## Medium Priority
- Hunting spawn competition.
- Trucking scaling with player saturation.
- Diving radio restriction hurting social play.

# Strongest Takeaways
1. Economy bottlenecks are poorly distributed.
   - Gold abundance vs jewelry part scarcity.

2. Time investment feels disproportionate.
   - Especially processing/crafting.

3. Players optimize hard toward best-paying jobs.
   - Weak activities become abandoned quickly.

4. Social friction reduces engagement.
   - Diving radio restriction cited repeatedly by players.

5. The player values balance through buffs, not nerfs.
   - Improve alternatives instead of destroying profitable loops.
